About:

The story behind this project starts in 2019, when I was a student in the data science program at Northeastern University. We were in the midst of a section on K Means clustering, which I was already familiar with, but what stuck out to me this time was the application to images. By applying K Means clustering to a flattened image array, I learned you could essentially render an images' pixels down into groupings that corresponded to objects in the original photo. But nevermind the obvious utility this has to the field of computer vision, I just thought it was really cool. In a way, I found it was sort of like looking at an Andy Warhol painting.

After graduating in 2020 amidst the pandemic, I found myself facing a problem: as newly minted data scientist with little actual work experience, I was finding it very difficult to actually convince people I had experience working with machine learning algorithms, which as one can imagine, isn't great when trying to find employment. I was eventually able to find work as a business analyst, which occupied my time for about two years before I was laid off in 2023 when funding ran out.

That massively sucked, but there was a silver-lining: with all the freetime I now had, I decided it was good time to build this website and kill two birds with one stone: show the world I can work with machine learning algorithms; demonstrate my ability as a programmer to build and deploy a functioning website; and most importantly, just do something fun and educational.

This image gallery website is the result of these efforts. It's a space devoted to showing some of the more interesting results of the images from my life, post-image segmentation. I intend this to be a living project that will continue to expand and thrive overtime, and I hope you find the result of my work to be as interesting as I do.
